Things To Do!

Astoria Park

Astoria Park is a one of a kind. While it may not be as grand as parks such as Central Park or Prospect Park, it still has its own little spark. For both aforementioned parks, they are heavily populated meaning they atract tons of visitor everyday. This aspect is much different from Astoria Park's environment. Its small community leaves only a few people walking the windy paths it has. If you're looking for a peaceful, yet large, park, then Astoria Park is just for you. It even contains a path along the East River, leaving tons of room for beautiful sunset pictures with the bridge.

The Museum of Moving Images

The Museum of Moving Images definitely isn't as large as The Museum of Natural History, but it is big enough to fit a 1-2 hour agenda. If you're looking for a small museum to attend in hopes of gainging some knowledge, specifically on the art, history,technique, and technology of film, televsion, or even the digital media, then you've come to the right place. With countless exhibitons on such topics, you'll surely leave with your brain bigger than ever. They also conduct screenings of films, presenting more than 400 films, whether that may be of silent films or just random clips, they have it all.

Astoria's Carnivals/ Fairs

One thing that the community of Astoria knows a ton about is it's vast amount of public carnivals and fairs that are typically held multiple times a year. Whether it is for some sort of celebration, or nothing at all, you can be assured that it will prove worthy of your time. With their amazing street fairs, you'll get the chance to witness the unity that Astoria is all about. With different cultural food vendors left and right, you're bound to join in on the fun.
